I made this website and documented the process.

So, I decided to make a website. I am barely literate with html, css and JS, but I had a secret weapon: the internet. Also a friend who is an incredible web developer. I am incredibly grateful for his constructive criticism and bug-fixing the almost unintelligible code I wrote.

The original idea and layout of my site.

Originally, the idea behind my site was completely different. I liked this picture and though it would be a good starting point for my design. It made a decent first page, but I didn’t like where it led me when I was trying to come up with ideas for the other pages. Ie: I had no ideas for the other pages.

I decided that I would redo my site and move away from having a single image as my background. It didn’t scale well and I ended up having to jump through a lot of hoops in order to get something I was ok with. Instead, I decided I should put a model of my 3d printer (CR10) on the first page, but not use it as the background. I am a big fan of 3d printing, and I wanted to use this page to show off my 3D modeling talent.

Fun fact, in an earlier draft of my site, the printer would “print” if you scrolled up and down. This was achieved with a few dozen clever div elements and way too much time. I ended up scrapping it because it refused to scale well and was more trouble than its worth.

Now that I had my base idea down, I needed a color pallet to work on. I am pretty bad with colors, but my friend had a few good site recommendations and I found my inspiration.

Botanical Hues from design-seeds.com

I ended up picking a few colors from this pallet and ran with them.

Now, all I had to do was to model my printer and export it Blender to render it.

blender renders and maybe some prog pictures

With that finished, I put the assets into my site, and it really started to come together. After finishing the first page, I realized that I had too much empty space, so I rendered some of my other projects and put them in to fill the space and give the viewer a better idea of who I am.

The final-ish design for my coverpage. It probably looks a little differnt because I am always tweaking my designs.

With that finished and a clear path for the rest of the site, I blocked out the rest of the pages and got to work.

Wireframe design of my "Projects" page, complete with a few notes.
