A little bit about me.

Who I am

Covid-19 stole my summer internship, so I decided to use that time to learn the basics of at least one programming language. I ended up learning basic C++, html, CSS, and some JavaScript. I mention this because I think it is a good example of what type of person I am. Before the shutdown, I only knew how to work with Matlab and felt like my ignorance of programming was one of my weaknesses, so I started fixing it. Now, I have a better understanding of the inner workings of programming, and I can contribute when people are talking about their projects. Or at least, I can follow along with what they are doing. In addition, learning C++ has let me start to add automation to my designs, which is extremely fun. Eventually, I want to fill my house with over-engineered gadgets that do everything for me, starting with a robotic desk light.

As you go through the projects on my site, you may notice the underlying goal behind all of them (aside from being fun to make). Each project I do is to test my abilities and teach me new skills. At the end of the day, I really like making stuff and I want to do it to the best of my abilities.
